A new branding website for the European Market

Euro-Floors is a shop located in Szczecin, Poland that specializes in high-quality laminate flooring sales. They have an online webshop to provide their products across the country.



CHALLENGE

The company is initially focused on Polish customers, so their webshop is in Polish, shows prices in Złoty and displays shipping costs inland. Throughout the years they started getting more European customers who place their orders through phone calls as they cannot make use of the webshop.



SOLUTION

A branding website that is targeted to potential European customers. The website focuses on raising the users’ interest and gaining their trust in the company, their products and services.

MY ROLES

Content Production, Front-End Development



LOCATION

Szczecin, Poland

View Prototype

DISCOVERING MARKET TRENDS

Aside from the usual user and client research that we conduct, I proposed to my team to include market research as well. Not only does this create context to our project, but it also allows us to discover market trends and consumer behaviour that could be relevant for content production.


From market reports we’ve found that the bathroom and kitchen are the most popular spaces that go through a home renovation. This resulted in the usage of images of these spaces on the website.


In addition, we conducted qualitative research to gain understanding about how both experienced and inexperienced users shop for laminate flooring: price and brands are their starting points when they look for laminate flooring online, so we gave brand names and product prices relatively more visual weight.

EXPERIMENTING WITH CSS NAMING CONVENTIONS

I’ve only known about one naming convention that was taught in class, but I was rather confused about it. So I took this project as an opportunity to get new knowledge in, practice with it and apply it.


There are a lot of different CSS naming conventions out there and the BEM is one that I understand and find efficient. The code that I wrote is therefore highly influenced by this naming convention. I was mostly fascinated by the modifier element where you use double dashes and I've used this method for other projects ever since.
